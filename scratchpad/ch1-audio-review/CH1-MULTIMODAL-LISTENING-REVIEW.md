# Chapter 1 Multimodal Listening Review

## Final decision

**Winner: V1.**

Use V1 to replace the full closing clause:

> **to ensure safe passage through spiritual realms**

The splice should begin immediately after **“curandero”**, at the sentence’s natural pause, and continue through **“spiritual realms.”**

## Listening findings

### CURRENT

The accepted Chapter 1 performance is consistent before and after the defect, but the location where **“to ensure”** belongs breaks into prolonged garbled, unintelligible speech. It cannot remain in the final audiobook.

### V1

V1 speaks the full clause clearly. Its voice, timbre, pitch, cadence, pacing, emotional tone, pronunciation, loudness, and recording texture are the closest match to CURRENT. The clause carries the correct inflection and rhythm and offers the lowest perceptual seam risk when entered after “curandero.”

### V2

V2 also speaks “to ensure” clearly, but it sounds slightly higher, breathier, and slower than CURRENT. That difference is more noticeable and creates a greater risk of an audible voice change at the splice.

## Why replace the full clause rather than only two words

Cutting only **“to ensure”** would require two very tight seams around a short phrase and would preserve the disturbed timing created by the long breakdown. Replacing the entire clause from the natural pause after **“curandero”** through the sentence ending gives one clean entrance and a natural sentence-ending exit.

## Implementation constraint

Perform the edit from the original/lossless source material, not from the 128 kbps review MP3. After the splice, audition both seams on headphones and speakers before rebuilding the combined audiobook.

## Relationship to the numerical report

The automated acoustic/transcription report selected V2 using a composite numerical score. The multimodal listening review evaluated the audible voice character and seam perception directly and selected V1. For an audiobook edit, the direct listening determination governs the final choice.
